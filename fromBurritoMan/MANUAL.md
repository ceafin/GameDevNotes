# BURRITO MAN
### and the Fight for the Flavor of the World

*Instruction Booklet | For home play | Original recipe, no artificial ingredients*

> Keep this booklet. It holds the story, the controls, and a few secrets from the test kitchen.

## THE STORY

Once, every corner of the world cooked its own way. Smoke and spice and slow simmering. Streets that smelled like home. Nobody wrote it down because nobody had to. It was just *dinner*, and it was perfect.

Then came **The Franchise.**

It arrived quietly, one identical storefront at a time, promising food that was faster, cheaper, and exactly the same everywhere. Its founder, **Burger Bob**, had one dream: a single flavor for the whole planet. His flavor. He called it progress. Everyone else called it lunch, until it was too late.

The Franchise did not just open next door. It **spread.** Its grease seeped into the food itself, and the dishes of each world twisted into hollow, hostile things. Loyal noodles, dumplings, and pastries turned to snarling foot soldiers. The great guardians of each cuisine were corrupted into monsters that guarded their own ruined kitchens. And the heroes who might have fought back? Sealed away, one to a world, silenced.

Six worlds fell. The Franchise's home, a monstrous mall called **The Food Court**, glowed on the horizon like a heat lamp that never turns off.

But grease misses a spot.

In the Mexican Food World, wrapped tight and slow-cooked to perfection, one small hero was ready. Chili blaster loaded. Foil folded sharp. He does not want to rule the world.

He just wants to give it its flavor back.

**You are BURRITO MAN.**

> **Important:** You do not destroy the guardians. They are good food, only corrupted. Beat one and the grease burns away, the guardian is **freed**, and its world blooms back to life. Every victory is a rescue.

## YOUR HERO

### Burrito Man
A humble burrito with an enormous heart and a hotter temper. His **Chili Blaster** fires bolts of pure capsaicin heat, the one thing The Franchise cannot mass-produce: actual spice. In his base form he is small, quick, and slips through gaps the big enemies cannot follow.

### Burrito Supremo (Super Mode)
Press **Transform** and Burrito Man wraps up into **Burrito Supremo**: bigger, bolder, cape snapping in the wind, eyes masked for business. This is the fighting form. Only in Supremo can he **charge** the Chili Blaster and wield the borrowed powers of the champions he frees.

Power has a price. Supremo runs on the **Flavor Gauge**. It drains while you hold the form and empties faster when you fire borrowed powers. Run dry and you snap back to plain Burrito Man until it refills. Bigger form, bigger target, so pick your moments.

**Read the cape.** Its color, and the mask, tell you which power is loaded:

| Cape / Mask color | Power loaded |
|---|---|
| Red (default) | Charged Chili Blaster |
| (see The Powers) | one of the six freed champion powers |

### Quesadilla Kid
Burrito Man's little partner, holding down the Mexican Food World while you are out saving everyone else's dinner. Between worlds, the Kid patches you up, keeps the salsa hot, and cheers louder than anyone. Small, folded, unstoppably optimistic.

## HOW TO PLAY

### Controls

| Input | Action |
|---|---|
| D-Pad Left / Right | Run |
| D-Pad Up / Down | Climb ladders, aim on ladders |
| Jump | Jump (hold higher, tap for a short hop) |
| Down + Jump | Slide (nips under fire and through low gaps) |
| Fire | Shoot the Chili Blaster (hold in Supremo to charge) |
| Transform | Toggle Burrito Man <-> Burrito Supremo |
| Start | Pause + Power Select menu |
| Select | (reserved) |

### The two forms, at a glance

| | Base | Supremo |
|---|---|---|
| Charge shot | no | yes |
| Champion powers | no | yes |
| Size / hitbox | small, tight gaps | large |
| Costs Flavor | no | yes |

### Firing rules
- Up to **three** shots live on screen at once. A charged shot counts as one of the three.
- **Charge** only in Supremo: hold Fire to build heat, release for a bigger bolt.
- Switch loaded power any time from the **Start** menu. Powers only fire in Supremo.

### Staying alive
- Your **Health Bar** is your heat. Take a hit, you flash and get knocked back, briefly untouchable while you recover. Use those moments.
- **Pits and spikes are instant.** No health check, no mercy. Watch your footing.
- The **Flavor Gauge** feeds Supremo. Keep it topped up before a boss door.

## THE SIX WORLDS

The Franchise corrupted six great cuisines. Each world hides a captured **champion**. Free them and you learn to cook the way they do: their power becomes yours. Every corrupted guardian fears exactly one flavor, so the order you travel in matters.

| World | Corrupted Guardian (Boss) | Champion to Free | Power You Gain |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chinese Food World | Kung Pao Chow | Jia the Jianbing | Five-Spice Scatter |
| Japanese Food World | Ramen Ryuu | Emi the Ehomaki | Maki Roller |
| Palestinian Food World | Falafel Phil | Shadi the Shawarma | Shawarma Spin |
| Indian Food World | Sammy Samosa | Kartik the Kati Roll | Tandoor Torch |
| French Food World | Ratatouille Roux | Claude the Crepe Salee | Crepe Cutter |
| Italian Food World | The Polpette Posse | Carlo the Calzone | Calzone Crush |

### Chinese Food World
Steam, woks, and a sky full of drifting peppercorns gone rancid. **Kung Pao Chow** rules a corrupted banquet hall, flinging bursts of numbing pepper. Free **Jia**, master of the folded street crepe, and learn the **Five-Spice Scatter**.

### Japanese Food World
A neon night market twisted into a maze of conveyor belts. Sushi Samurais patrol, Maki Rollers barrel down the lanes, and the mini-boss **Onigiri Oni** guards the way, a little rice ball turned big folklore ogre. At the top coils **Ramen Ryuu**, a noodle dragon of scalding broth. Free **Emi** and gain the piercing **Maki Roller**.

### Palestinian Food World
Sun-baked stone, spinning spits, and the endless drum of **Falafel Phil**, whose fried chickpea soldiers never break rank. Free **Shadi**, whose spit spins forever, and gain the **Shawarma Spin**, a shield of roasted shavings that orbits and blocks.

### Indian Food World
Tandoor fires roar out of control, and **Sammy Samosa** rains fried pastry from a throne of embers. Free **Kartik**, the flame-wrapped roll, and learn the **Tandoor Torch**, a high-arcing fireball that drops on grounded foes.

### French Food World
A grand kitchen turned haunted, copper pots bubbling on their own. **Ratatouille Roux**, a cauldron of animated vegetables, guards the pass. Free **Claude**, the savory crepe, and gain the **Crepe Cutter**, a thin folded disc that flies out and loops back.

### Italian Food World
Checkered floors and a crew that made you an offer. **The Polpette Posse** works together, no lone boss, all rolling muscle. Breadsticks, Calamari, and cold-eyed Gelato run the streets. Free **Carlo**, the sealed and stuffed calzone, and gain the **Calzone Crush**, a heavy close-range slam that flattens the toughest guard.

Each freed champion teaches Burrito Supremo a new way to fight. Every power has its shape.

| Power | Cape signals | Behaves like | Best against |
|---|---|---|---|
| Five-Spice Scatter | (Chinese palette) | three-way spread | crowds and swarms |
| Maki Roller | (Japanese palette) | pierces in a straight line | rows of enemies, shielded foes |

| Tandoor Torch | (Indian palette) | high arc, drops down | grounded and low targets |
| Crepe Cutter | (French palette) | flies out and returns | hitting on the way there and back |

## THE FORTRESS: THE FOOD COURT

Past the six worlds waits the source of the rot. Neon arches, endless queues, a drive-thru that circles forever. The ground is fry-grease and the air hums with a jingle you cannot un-hear. Everything here is red, white, and blue. Not a flag. A logo. The same three colors stamped on every tray, cup, and napkin.

The Franchise throws its house army at you: skittering **Fries**, gnashing **Nuggets**, and coiled **Hot Dogs** that spring from every vent. It cannot cook the real thing, so deeper in it sends cheap **off-brand knockoffs** of the guardians you already freed, hollow imitations with none of the soul.

At the top of it all sits **Burger Bob**, founder of The Franchise, dreaming his single grey dream for the whole world. He built a machine to erase every flavor but his own. Only one thing was too spicy to process.

Give the world back its flavor. All of it.

## TIPS FROM THE TEST KITCHEN

- **Base form is a tool, not a punishment.** Small and quick beats big and armed when the room is all spikes and narrow gaps. Drop out of Supremo on purpose.
- **Every guardian fears one flavor.** If a boss is barely flinching, you are using the wrong power. Try a different one.
- **Charge before the door.** A full Chili Blaster charge on frame one of a boss fight is free damage.
- **Read the cape.** Never guess which power is loaded. The color tells you.
- **Slide under, not over.** Some fire you cannot jump. You can always slide.

## APPENDIX: DESIGNER'S NOTES (out of fiction)

Not part of the player booklet. Consequences of the locked narrative + Mode Model X (no flight), flagged so the manual and the build stay honest.

| Note | Detail |
|---|---|
| New input action | "Transform" is not in the roadmap's Phase 0 input map. Add it (`transform`) to the input map. |
| Roadmap lock edits | Model X consciously overrides two locks: charge is now Supremo-only (not always-on), and champion powers are gated behind Supremo. Update the roadmap's Locked Decisions #1 and the weapon-select flow to match, or they will drift. |
| Weapon-select scope | The Start/Pause power-select menu only *fires* in Supremo. Decide: does equipping a power in base mode auto-transform on next fire, or just queue it? Recommend: queue only, no auto-transform. |
| Base-form niche | The small-hitbox / tight-gap advantage only exists if levels actually build base-only gaps. If you never build them, base mode has no reason to exist. Budget a few base-only sections per stage. |
| Villain reframe | Final world is **The Food Court** (corporate chain), not any nation. Brand palette is red/white/blue as a logo, explicitly not a flag. No real-world country is the villain. |
| Purify, not destroy | Guardians are freed, not killed. Fortress refights are **off-brand knockoffs**, not the real (now-freed) guardians. Keeps the lore consistent. |
| Naming | Playful/rhyming names only, no military or royal titles. Mini-boss softened from a crime trope to **Onigiri Oni**. |
| Weakness ring is tunable | The ring below is a plausible single-loop starting point, not canon. Tune freely. |
