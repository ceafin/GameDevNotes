# Celtic ARPG Mechanics — Merged Summary, Corrections & Sanity Check

This consolidates the three mechanics files into one reference, flags specific factual errors about the reference games, and sanity-checks the design against your stated direction: **Zelda-like + Valkyrie Profile-like action RPG/JRPG.** That combination wasn't part of the source files' assumptions, so §5 is new analysis, not a correction of something they got wrong.

## 1. What each source file is

- **Base Collection.md** — the raw pull: every notable item/verb from 7 reference games (ALttP, Zelda NES, Link's Awakening, Tunic, Prodigal, Mina the Hollower, Isle of Reveries), dumped into 15 flat categories (weapons, shields, movement, consumables, etc.), one bullet per item.
- **Base Collection in Theme.md** — the same material re-cut into 19 cross-game *archetypes* (e.g. "boomerang-likes," "strength/lifting gear") with a "core pattern" and per-game "takeaway" for each — the analysis pass on top of the raw dump.
- **Base Collection Matrix.md** — the actual design decisions for your project (called **Nasc** / *Finsceal Bhríde* in this file): each archetype tagged MUST-HAVE / NICE-TO-HAVE / OPTIONAL, plus a Gaelic (mostly Brigid/Brigidine) reskin for each — the synthesis pass.

## 3. Geasa: the one genuinely novel system

Not a direct pull from any reference game — it's the Matrix file's synthesis of the passive-trinket archetype (#11) with Irish/Scottish *geis/geasa* (personal taboo-obligations that grant power when kept and ruin when broken). This is correctly identified as your strongest "not just Zelda in Irish clothes" hook. Sketch: 2–3 equippable geas slots, each tied to a relic, each showing a buff + a taboo condition; breaking it trips a curse meter (tougher enemies, Sluagh spawns, XP loss). Examples given: Geis of Hospitality, Geis of the Hound (a direct, accurate callback to Cú Chulainn's own dog-geis), Geis of the Tide-Horse, Geis of Bríd's Fire.

## 4. Factual corrections

Confidence varies a lot by game — Nintendo's mainline titles and Tunic are well-documented; Prodigal, Mina the Hollower, and Isle of Reveries are small/recent indies I can't verify with the same certainty. Flagged accordingly.

**High confidence:**
- **"Bomb Arrows" attributed to Link's Awakening** (Base Collection.md §1, §12; also referenced in Base Collection in Theme.md §3/§4) — I don't believe original Link's Awakening (GB, DX, or the Switch remake) has a bow+bomb combo item called Bomb Arrows. That mechanic isn't part of LA's actual item set as far as I can verify. Worth double-checking against a wiki before it anchors a design decision — if it turns out not to exist, it's still a fine invented tech for Nasc, just don't treat it as "Zelda already validated this."
- **ALttP Magic Mirror described as leaving a "one-use portal"** (Base Collection.md §4) — the portal the Mirror leaves behind in the Dark World is reusable back-and-forth while you stay near it, not a single-use warp. Minor, but the Matrix's "panic recall charm" design (§C, Teleport/fast travel row) inherits this framing — worth deciding on purpose whether your version is one-shot or a standing portal, rather than assuming the Zelda precedent was one-shot.

**Needs verification (moderate confidence I'm right, but don't stake a design decision on it without checking):**
- **Link's Awakening trading-sequence NPC names** (Base Collection.md §10: "Ribbon → given to CiaoCiao," "Dog Food → given to Sale the crocodile," "Honeycomb → given to Chef Bear") — the *sequence* of 14 trade items (Yoshi Doll → Ribbon → Dog Food → Bananas → Stick → Honeycomb → Pineapple → Hibiscus → Letter → Broom → Fishing Hook → Necklace → Mermaid's Scale → Magnifying Lens) matches what I know, but several of the specific NPC names attached to each step don't match my recollection confidently enough to call them right or wrong. Since you're reskinning all of this into Gaelic NPCs anyway, it may not matter — but if you want it as an accurate reference for pacing/structure, re-verify the NPC list against a wiki rather than trusting it as-is.

**Low confidence — treat as unverified source material, not fact:**
- **Prodigal** (item names like Coin of Crowl, Bolt Anchor, Boots of Graile, the full Ring/Blessing lists) — this is a small enough indie title that I can't independently confirm these details. They read as internally consistent, which is a good sign, but I can't sanity-check them against the real game.
- **Mina the Hollower** (Whisper & Vesper, Volt Hatchet, Gyro Dagger, Buckler Driver, Pneumatic Armlet, exact burrow mechanics) — recent/niche release; I recognize the game and its general burrow-and-swap-weapons concept but can't verify item-level specifics.
- **Isle of Reveries** (the copy/paste terrain system) — I have no independent knowledge of this title; the mechanic as described is plausible and internally coherent but entirely unverified on my end.

None of this is disqualifying — you're reskinning everything into Gaelic myth regardless, so a slightly-wrong Zelda trivia detail doesn't break anything. It matters only where the Matrix file uses a specific mechanical detail (like "one-use portal") to justify a specific Nasc design choice.

## 5. Sanity check: Zelda-like *and* Valkyrie Profile-like

This is the part the source files don't address at all, since they were written assuming a pure Zelda-like. Worth naming the tension directly before you build around it.

**The core structural conflict.** Zelda's loop is: one hero, top-down/3D overworld, item-gated dungeons, single-character real-time combat with block/dodge. Valkyrie Profile's loop is: a *roster* of party members each mapped to a face button, side-view platforming through symbolic dungeons, combo-timing combat where you chain multiple characters' attacks to juggle enemies for extra hits, plus a meta-layer of **recruiting dead heroes (Einherjar) via their own flashback vignettes** and periodically dispatching them to Valhalla for permanent stat rewards. These are not small differences — VP's combat identity *is* the multi-character combo system, and its narrative identity *is* the nonlinear "recruit a doomed soul, replay their death, decide when to send them on" structure. You can't bolt that onto a single-hero Zelda skeleton without deciding which parts you actually want.

**Recommended read, not a mandate:** the cheapest, most coherent hybrid is *Zelda structure for exploration, VP structure for combat encounters and character-acquisition* — keep the single top-down hero and item-gated dungeons for traversal, but let combat drop into (or simply always feature) 2–4 companions fighting alongside you, each with their own weapon-archetype from your table in §2 (this is already implicitly supported — Mina's multi-weapon-choice pattern in row 1 is functionally a VP-lite already), with face-button-mapped attacks for combo timing. Full VP-style side-view platforming dungeons would mean building and maintaining two entirely different movement/camera systems — that's a much bigger ask than it sounds and I'd flag it as the single riskiest scope item if it's on the table.

**A genuinely good synthesis opportunity:** the Einherjar system maps onto your Geasa system almost too well to pass up. VP's einherjar are dead heroes with tragic pasts you recruit by resolving their story, then choose when to release to a higher power for reward — that's structurally close to "recruit a Sídhe-bound or geis-bound spirit, learn their story, decide whether to keep them bound to service (an ongoing companion with their own geis) or release them to the Otherworld/Tír na nÓg for a permanent blessing." This would give you VP's best narrative device (short, self-contained tragic vignettes) wired directly into the mythology you've already built (§C/§E of the Matrix file), rather than being two unrelated systems awkwardly coexisting.

**Two concrete design-clarity issues to fix regardless of the VP question:**
- **Geis of the Tide-Horse is currently unimplementable as written.** "Never turn your back to deep water in combat (if you roll away from an enemy toward land, you're fine; roll toward the shore with back to water – violation → ghostly tide surges)" conflates two different axes — which direction you roll, and which way you're facing — and the parenthetical example contradicts the stated rule (rolling *toward* shore is moving *away* from water, not turning your back to it). Before this goes into a design doc as implementable, it needs a single trackable condition, e.g. "your character's back-facing angle points at deep water while an enemy is within N tiles."
- **Scope math.** The Matrix's own TL;DR already commits to 10 "non-negotiable" pillars for a Zelda-like built by what reads like a small/solo team (all your comps — Tunic, Prodigal, Mina, Isle of Reveries — are small-team indies). Adding VP's multi-character combo combat, per-character stat/equipment tracking, and a recruitment-vignette narrative structure on top of that is a second full project's worth of systems. If you want both halves for real, I'd pick a phase 1 (Zelda exploration + geasa + a *simplified* 2-character combo swap, no full VP roster yet) and treat full Einherjar-style recruitment/dispatch and deep item-alchemy crafting as an explicit phase 2 stretch goal, rather than all ten-plus-VP pillars as day-one scope.
