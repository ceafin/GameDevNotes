## 1. Primary Melee Weapons (the “main sword” slot)

**Core pattern:** close-range, always-available attack that can be upgraded; often gains beams or special slashes.

- **Zelda-style swords (classic “main sword”)**
    - **ALttP - Fighter’s / Master / Tempered / Golden Sword**  
        Main melee line, 4 upgrades; Master+ enable **sword beams at full health** and medallion usage. Big late-game damage scaling.
    - **Zelda NES - Wooden / White / Magical Sword**  
        Same concept: 3-step upgrade line gated by heart containers; **all can shoot beams at full hearts**, Magical Sword is endgame.
    - **LA - Sword L-1 / Seashell Sword L-2**  
        2-step line; L-2 has **stronger damage plus sword beams at full hearts**, charged spin available from the start.
    - **IoR - Sword**  
        Simple main melee sword; no documented multi-tier progression in this list, more like “baseline Link sword” without beams.
    **Shared archetype:** permanent main melee weapon with upgrade steps and occasional beams.  
    **Differences:** number of upgrade tiers (3–4 in ALttP/Zelda NES vs 2 in LA vs 1 in IoR), and which tiers actually get beams (all in Zelda NES, high tiers only in ALttP/LA).
- **Tunic - Stick → Sword**
    - **Stick**: weak starter melee.
    - **Sword**: replaces stick as main sword; **no beams**, but combat depth comes from **stamina, rolls, thrust tech, and card modifiers** rather than pure upgrades.
- **Prodigal - Pickaxe line**
    - **Pickaxe / Blessed Pickaxe / Cursed Pickaxe / Bolt Anchor**: the pickaxe is effectively the “sword,” with **4-direction swings**, charge attacks, and later extreme damage (**Bolt Anchor**). Cursed/Blessed variants adjust power and penalties instead of beams.
    - Functionally the same archetype (primary melee with upgrades) but **framed as mining gear** and tuned heavily around **charged AoE swings**.
- **Mina - Nightstar / Battery Buster / Blaststrike Maul / Whisper & Vesper / Hollower’s Shield**
    - **Nightstar** = default “balanced sword” (mace-whip) with 4-direction coverage.
    - **Blaststrike Maul** = slow, huge-damage equivalent of a heavy “biggoron” sword.
    - **Battery Buster** = alternate main weapon with its own moveset.
    - **Whisper & Vesper** = dual-dagger main weapon: very fast slashes, plus charge-throw and retrieval.
    - **Hollower’s Shield** = shield-as-weapon main type.  
        Here the main weapon slot is **multi-choice**, but all fill the “main melee” archetype via damage, reach, and moveset.

**Takeaway:**  
All of these games give you a **persistent, upgradable primary melee weapon**, sometimes with beams (ALttP, Zelda NES, LA), sometimes with deeper **moveset & system hooks** instead (Tunic stamina, Prodigal charge AoE, Mina weapon variety).

## 2. Returning / Stun Weapons (Boomerang-likes)

**Core pattern:** low-damage projectile that returns, stuns, and grabs items.

- **Boomerangs**
    - **ALttP - Boomerang / Magical Boomerang**  
        Short vs longer/faster version; **stuns** and pulls pickups.
    - **Zelda NES - Boomerang / Magical Boomerang**  
        Almost the same as ALttP: short vs full-screen version; **stun focus**.
    - **LA - Boomerang**  
        Secret late-game, but serves the same stun + item retrieval role; damage is **very high** vs earlier Zeldas.
- **Conceptual cousins**
    - **Mina - Gyro Dagger**  
        Throwing dagger that behaves like a **boomerang-style projectile** (returns, can be juggled/whipped mid-flight).
    - **Tunic - Magic Dagger (when thrown) + Grapple**  
        Not literally a boomerang, but **repeatable short-range projectile** with status (freeze) + a grapple that pulls enemies/objects.
    - **Prodigal - Lariat (as attack)**  
        Rope that can also **strike at range** as it grabs/drag objects.

**Takeaway:**  
Classic Zeldas treat the **boomerang as a stun + utility tool**; later games riff on that with **returning daggers or ropes** that combine stun, pull, and positional control.

## 3. Bombs & Explosives

**Core pattern:** limited-ammo explosives that damage enemies and open hidden terrain.

- **Standard bombs**
    - **ALttP - Bombs**: place/throw, blow up cracked walls and enemies.
    - **Zelda NES - Bombs**: same use, more guess-heavy dungeon walls.
    - **LA - Bombs**: same again; plus combos like bomb jumping and…
    - **LA - Bomb Arrows**: overlay Bow + Bomb to create **explosive arrows** for precision demolition.
    - **Tunic - Blast / Explosive Bomb**: throwable bombs with small AoE; plus **Fire Bomb** (lingering flames) and **Frost Bomb** (freeze cloud).
- **Super bomb variants**
    - **ALttP - Super Bomb**: follow-you mega bomb used on **special cracks only** (e.g., Pyramid).

**Takeaway:**  
Bombs are nearly identical across Zeldas: **terrain + combat**. Tunic evolves the concept by **splitting bombs into multiple elemental types** (fire / frost / standard).

## 4. Bows, Arrows & “Silver Arrow” Kill-switch

**Core pattern:** ranged precision weapon; special silver/holy arrows as late-game Ganon killers.

- **Bow + arrows**
    - **ALttP - Bow, Arrows, Silver Arrows**  
        Arrows for switches and ranged damage; **Silver Arrows specifically required to kill Ganon.** Capacity upgrades.
    - **Zelda NES - Bow, Silver Arrows, Upgrade sword**  
        Bow needed for some bosses; **Silver Arrows again: only way to kill Ganon.**
    - **LA - Bow & Arrows, Arrows (ammo)**  
        Standard bow; capacity upgrades; can combine with bombs (Bomb Arrows) for extra tech.
- **Ranged equivalents**
    - **Tunic - Wand / Magic Rod, Gun / Shotgun**  
        Fill the role of “bow” as MP-gated ranged options rather than physical arrows.
    - **Prodigal - Rust Knuckle + Lariat + Pick charge**  
        Prodigal leans more on melee and AoE, but ranged precision is less central than in Zeldas.

**Takeaway:**  
The **“bow as baseline ranged tool” + “silver arrows as final boss key”** is a Zelda-specific pattern; other games replace it with MP-based guns, wands, or more melee-centric combat.

## 5. Elemental & Magic Projectile Tools

**Core pattern:** magic rods/wands that shoot fire/ice/beam projectiles, often doubling as puzzle tools.

- **Zelda NES**
    - **Magical Rod + Book of Magic**: rod shoots a magic beam; Book upgrades it into a **flame projectile**.
- **ALttP**
    - **Fire Rod**: long-range fire blast; burns enemies and torches.
    - **Ice Rod**: cold beam that **freezes enemies/obstacles**.
    - **Cane of Somaria / Byrna, Medallions**: screen-wide or area magic that double as puzzle nukes.
- **LA**
    - **Magic Rod**: continuous fire stream for combat and lighting torches.
    - **Magic Powder**: sprinkled dust that damages/stuns/transforms; puzzle triggers.
- **Tunic**
    - **Magic Dagger**: short ice burst, **freezes** enemies; MP-gated.
    - **Wand / Magic Rod**: fires ranged shots; acts as “rod analog.”
    - **Hourglass**: time-slow field; more systemic but still magic-cost AoE control.

**Takeaway:**  
All of these are **“spell slot” tools**: ranged, often elemental, used both for combat and environment. Differences are mostly in **shape (beam vs stream vs burst)** and **system hooks (MP vs magic meter vs stamina)**.

## 6. Shields & Armor / Damage Reduction

**Core pattern:** front-facing projectile defense, sometimes auto-blocking, sometimes manual with stamina; armor tiers as flat damage reduction.

- **Basic → Magical shields**
    - **Zelda NES - Wooden Shield / Magical Shield**: auto-block when facing projectiles; Magical blocks **stronger beams**.
    - **ALttP - Fighter’s Shield / Red Shield / Mirror Shield**: manual facing but similar concept; Mirror reflects **beams/lasers**.
    - **LA - Shield / Mirror Shield**: held to block contact and projectiles; Mirror reflects lasers.
- **Active stamina shields**
    - **Tunic - Shield**: **manual guard** that consumes stamina; enables **parries** and card-modified behavior (Bracer, Aura’s Gem, etc.).
- **Armor / tunics**
    - **ALttP - Green Clothes → Blue Mail → Red Mail**: discrete big jumps in damage reduction.
    - **Prodigal - Anchor Greaves, Golem Ring, Oakley’s Ring, etc.**: armor is more modular; defensive boots + rings tradeoff damage vs armor.
    - **Mina - Defense trinkets**: passive trinkets that tweak damage taken.
- **Offensive shield hybrid**
    - **Mina - Hollower’s Shield**: unique twist: shield as **main weapon type**, not just defense.

**Takeaway:**  
The **Zelda line evolves into more active defense in Tunic**, and Mina flips it further into offensive style. Armor shifts from simple tunic tiers (ALttP) to **modular tradeoff items** (Prodigal, trinkets in Mina/Tunic).

## 7. Boots, Speed & Movement Enhancers

**Core pattern:** equip boots or passives that increase speed, change footing, or grant special dash.

- **Dash / sprint boots**
    - **ALttP - Pegasus Boots**: charge then dash for speed, dash attacks, break obstacles.
    - **LA - Pegasus Boots**: same idea; combine with **Feather** for long jumps.
    - **Prodigal - Winged Boots**: hold run for **much faster movement**.
    - **Prodigal - Rocket Boots (+ Flare Knuckle)**: turns punches into **full-screen rocket dashes with i-frames**.
    - **Tunic - Anklet**: passive **move-speed boost** rather than explicit dash.
- **Special footing / knockback boots**
    - **Prodigal - Cleated Boots**: ignore ice, give +1 damage.
    - **Prodigal - Anchor Greaves**: reduce knockback, more tanky footing.
    - **Boots of Graile, Lucky Boots**: stat-boost or luck-focused variants.

**Takeaway:**  
Boots are universally the **“mobility modifier” slot**. Zelda keeps it to “run + break stuff,” Prodigal extends into **build-defining mobility options** (rocket dashes, knockback resistance), Tunic folds speed into a card-like trinket.

## 8. Strength / Lifting Gear

**Core pattern:** an equip/upgrade that lets you lift heavier objects over time.

- **ALttP - Power Glove → Titan’s Mitt**  
    Progressively allow lifting **light rocks** then **heavy dark rocks**, unlocking more map.
- **Zelda NES - Power Bracelet**  
    Lets you **push** specific stones for warp tunnels; less about weight, more about secrets.
- **LA - Power Bracelet L-1 / L-2**  
    Lift pots, skulls, small rocks at L-1; heavy stones/statues at L-2.

**Takeaway:**  
Same archetype: **strength-gating terrain**, with LA being the cleanest 2-tier version; ALttP similar; Zelda NES is more “warp unlock” flavour.

## 9. Water & Environmental Access (Swim, Raft, Breathing)

**Core pattern:** gear that lets you traverse deep water, hazardous zones, or cross water gaps.

- **Swimming gear**
    - **ALttP - Zora’s Flippers**: swim in deep water, reach watery areas.
    - **LA - Flippers**: swim plus **dive** for secrets and dodging.
- **Zelda NES - Raft + Stepladder**
    - **Raft** crosses big water via docks.
    - **Stepladder** crosses one-tile water/lava gaps.
- **Prodigal - Bandana**  
    Lets you **breathe underwater / in toxic zones** and is required for certain mini-games.
- **Mina - Burrow** (not water but comparable gating)  
    Lets you **go underground**, bypass obstacles and some hazards, acting as “terrain bypass gear.”

**Takeaway:**  
All are “**hazard traversal unlocks**.” Zelda splits water into raft vs flippers vs ladder; LA adds a dive layer; Prodigal/Mina stretch the pattern into **hazard immunity or subterranean routing.**

## 10. Grapples, Hooks & Teleport Recalls

**Core pattern:** repositioning tools: either you move to something (grapple) or warp back to a point.

- **Hookshot / Grapple**
    - **ALttP - Hookshot**: grapple to targets, cross gaps, stun enemies.
    - **LA - Hookshot**: same concept; pulls Link and pulls objects, doubles as stun/damage.
    - **Tunic - Grapple**: zip to hooks or **pull enemies/frozen targets**.
    - **Prodigal - Lariat**: grapples to tethers, **drags objects, crosses gaps, hits far switches**.
- **Teleport / recall**
    - **ALttP - Magic Mirror + Flute**  
        Mirror warps Dark→Light and creates a **temporary portal back**; Flute for multi-point fast travel.
    - **Zelda NES - Recorder (Flute) + Power Bracelet warps**  
        Recorder warps between dungeons; Bracelet reveals warp tunnels.
    - **LA - Ocarina (Manbo’s Mambo)**  
        Song warps to pond/dungeon entrance.
    - **Prodigal - Dread Hand / Empowered Hand**  
        Warp to **room entrance**, later to a **placed shadow clone**; includes i-frames on recall.
    - **Tunic - Shrine warp**  
        Obelisk network fast-travel between shrines.

**Takeaway:**  
All games converge on “**position control tools**”:

- **Grapple** = local, line-of-sight mobility + object pulling.
- **Recalls/warps** = global fast travel or safety teleport (Prodigal’s Dread Hand is the most “panic button” version).

## 11. Passive Rings, Trinkets, Cards, Blessings

**Core pattern:** equipables that modify stats, economy, or mechanics without needing active use.

- **Prodigal - Rings & Blessings**
    - Rings: **Illusion Ring (stealth), Golem Ring (stone shield), Magnet Ring (auto-loot), Spiked Ring (+damage)**, Daemon’s Ring (barrier), etc.
    - Blessings (Life/Light/Storm/Earth/Water): mostly **gating & lore**, but in practice they also mark progression.
- **Tunic - Cards & Stat Items**
    - Cards like **Orange Peril Ring, Cyan Peril Ring, Tincture, Bracer, Aura’s Gem, Muffling Bell, Scavenger’s Mask, Bone**: mod combat, stamina, stealth, i-frames, etc.
    - Stat items (Tooth, Effigy statue, Flower, Leaf, Mushroom, Ash) permanently raise **ATT, DEF, HP, stamina, MP, potion potency.**
- **Mina - Trinkets & Bones**
    - Ferrying Wisp, Spider trinket, Pneumatic Armlet, burrow-time trinket, defense trinket, etc. modify **movement, throw power, survivability**.
    - Bones spent on **Attack, Defense, Sidearm, Vial capacity** at rest points.

**Takeaway:**  
All three (Prodigal, Tunic, Mina) share a modern trend: **buildcraft through small passive items**, versus classic Zeldas where passive upgrades are largely linear (tunic tiers, strength, etc).

## 12. Light & “Utility” Tools (Lanterns, Candles, Shovel, Mirror, etc.)

**Core pattern:** non-combat items that enable visibility or world interaction.

- **Light sources**
    - **ALttP - Lamp / Lantern**: lights dark rooms, torches; uses magic.
    - **Zelda NES - Blue Candle / Red Candle**: provide light + **burn bushes**; Red candle multiple uses per screen.
    - **Tunic - Lantern**: passive light aura.
    - **IoR - Torches via copy system**: can be copied/spawned to light multiple spots.
- **Digging / burrowing**
    - **ALttP - Shovel**: digs soft soil for items and story triggers.
    - **LA - Shovel (Digging Game)**: similar dig mechanic, used heavily in one side activity.
    - **Mina - Burrow**: the extreme evolution—becomes an entire **movement layer** instead of just a “search tile” mechanic.
- **World interaction utilities**
    - **ALttP - Book of Mudora, Magic Mirror (utility), Magic Powder, Bottles, Super Bomb**: reading tablets, Light/Dark puzzle routing, status transformations, portable containers.
    - **Zelda NES - Letter, Raft, Stepladder, Recorder**: unlock shop, cross water, warp.
    - **LA - Ocarina songs, Piece of Power / Guardian Acorn as temporary buffs.**
    - **Tunic - Manual pages** as meta-knowledge; huge part of the game’s “utility layer.”

**Takeaway:**  
Light + dig + “puzzle tools” are shared; Mina and Tunic lean hardest into **utility as a core identity** (burrow as navigation, manual as meta-mechanic).

## 13. Potions, Food & Temporary Buffs

**Core pattern:** consumables for healing, resource restore, or short-term buffs.

- **Pure healing / resource potions**
    - **ALttP - Red/Green/Blue Potions, Fairy in a bottle**: HP, magic, or both; fairy revive on death.
    - **Zelda NES - Life Potion / 2nd Potion**: 1–2 full heals.
    - **LA - Secret Medicine**: auto-revive.
    - **Tunic - Refillable Potions**, upgraded by **Ash**; also separate **Red/Blue Fruits** for HP/MP.
    - **Mina - Plasma Vials**, refill by damage dealt/checkpoints; mimic Estus-style flasks.
- **Food and buff items**
    - **Prodigal - Smoked Salmon, Spiced Ham, Frostberry Cobbler, Strength/Luck Potion, Fall Feather, Nightmare Urchin, Dream Sundae, Boon, Lovely Meal**:  
        Huge spread of **temporary HP, speed, damage, luck, reflect, invuln, revive** effects. Very build-heavy.
    - **Tunic - Hot Pepper, Ivy/Vine, Lure/Decoy, Effigy (money), etc.**: attack/defense/stamina buffs plus economy tools.
    - **Zelda NES - Food/Meat**: mostly a **puzzle/quest item** (bait) rather than a buff.

**Takeaway:**  
Older Zeldas focus on **simple heals + a single revive**, while Prodigal/Tunic/Mina use consumables as **full buff systems** and even economy tools.

## 14. Health, Magic & Stat Progression

**Core pattern:** permanent upgrades to HP and other stats, often via containers/pieces or shrine offerings.

- **Hearts / HP**
    - **ALttP - Heart Containers & Pieces of Heart**
    - **Zelda NES - Heart Containers, Recovery Hearts, Fairies**
    - **LA - Heart Containers, Heart Pieces, Big Hearts**
    - **Tunic - Flower (HP), Tooth (ATT), Figurine (DEF), etc.**
    - **Prodigal - Heart Ore → max HP up to 24**
    - **Mina - Vial Pouch & Bones → Attack/Defense/Vial capacity**
- **Magic / stamina**
    - **ALttP - Magic Upgrades 1/2 & 1/4** (effectively “magic cost rating”).
    - **Tunic - Mushroom (MP), Feathers/Leaves (stamina)**.
    - **Mina - Joules and Joule Jars** as ammo resource; trinkets & bones handle power.

**Takeaway:**  
The **Heart Container paradigm** is shared by all Zeldas; newer games generalize it into **multi-stat growth systems**, often tied to shrines, bones, or currency.

## 15. Keys, Maps, Compasses & MacGuffins

**Core pattern:** dungeon-internal keys for locks + high-level story macguffins that gate final content.

- **Dungeon keys & mapping**
    - **Zelda NES & ALttP & LA & Tunic**:
        - **Small Keys**: single-use door unlocks.
        - **Big/Nightmare/Magical Key**: boss doors or infinite key in NES.
        - **Maps & Compasses**: show layout, chest/boss locations; LA adds **Stone Beak** for hints.
    - **Prodigal - Normal/Crystal/Glass/Dusty/Divine/Ice Keys, Bone/Daemon’s/Prison/Bunny Keys**: same idea but with **more cosmetic and thematic variety**.
    - **IoR - LA-style key system**: rooms chime, HUD shows key presence; each dungeon has a **unique key item** to unlock content.
- **MacGuffins**
    - **ALttP - Three Pendants / Seven Crystals**
    - **Zelda NES - 8 Triforce Fragments**
    - **LA - 8 Instruments of the Sirens + Golden Leaves + Seashells**
    - **Prodigal - Blessings, Colors (Splinter, Frozen Heart, Shattered Soul, etc.)**
    - **Mina - Bones (for progression), story-specific keys**  
        These are all **“collect X big things to unlock final area/ending”** structures.

**Takeaway:**  
Dungeon keys/maps/compasses are nearly identical across all Zelda-likes; what changes per game is just the **number/shape of MacGuffins and how hard they’re tied into narrative vs pure gating.**

## 16. Currency & Economy

**Core pattern:** universal money used in shops and sometimes as “XP-like” resource.

- **Rupee-likes**
    - **ALttP, Zelda NES, LA - Rupees** (various colors/values).
    - **Tunic - Bits / Money crystals**, plus **Effigy** as “convert to cash later.”
    - **Prodigal - Gold**, tied into rings, shops, Golem Ring losing gold on hits.
    - **Mina - Bones (drop) + Bonestones/Bonesaw Dust (banked)**.
- **Special currencies**
    - **Prodigal - Coin of Crowl, Coin series** for Old Mines/Bolt Anchor.
    - **Tunic - Golden Coins tossed into wells**: not money but used to **unlock card slots**.

**Takeaway:**  
All games share a **single main currency**, but newer titles treat that as **both money and build currency** (Bones in Mina, Coins in Tunic as meta-unlock tokens).

## 17. Movement Verbs (Actions, independent of gear)

**Core pattern:** walk, run, jump, dash, climb, swim; plus game-specific twists.

- **Walk / run**
    - **ALttP**: 8-direction walk; dash with Pegasus Boots.
    - **Zelda NES, LA, IoR**: 4-direction walk; dash via boots (Zelda-likes) or jump combos.
    - **Tunic**: 8-way movement, **run by chaining roll**.
    - **Prodigal**: grid-style walk; Winged Boots/Spiced Ham for sprint.
    - **Mina**: standard platformer run + **burrow movement** as alt layer.
- **Jump / dash / roll**
    - **LA - Roc’s Feather**: one-tile jump; combo with **Pegasus Boots** for long jumps.
    - **IoR - Dedicated jump button**: always-available hop in combat/traversal.
    - **Tunic - Dodge Roll + Teleport Dash (Hero’s Laurels)**: central to combat and traversal.
    - **Mina - Basic jump + burrow exit jump**: extended jumps from underground.
- **Swim / climb / fall**
    - **ALttP/LA**: swim & climb; pits used for multi-floor nav.
    - **Zelda NES**: ladder for small gaps, raft for big water.
    - **Prodigal/Mina**: climbing gear, rope ladders; pits more about punishment or shortcuts.

**Takeaway:**  
The verbs are broadly shared; differences are **how heavily combat leans on them** (Tunic and Mina heavily integrate dash/jump/burrow into combat patterns).

## 18. Combat Verbs & Systems

**Core pattern:** basic swing, charged attack, projectile use, block or evade, plus some meta system for health recovery.

- **Basic + charged attacks**
    - **ALttP/LA**: sword slash + charged Spin Attack. LA adds **dash attack** and **jump attack**.
    - **Prodigal**: quick pick swings + **charged AoE spin**.
    - **Tunic**: light attack + **roll-thrust** for big damage.
    - **Mina**: 4-direction swings + **charged heavy attacks** for certain weapons.
- **Defense**
    - **Zelda NES/ALttP/LA**: shield facing; sometimes auto-block, sometimes manual.
    - **Tunic**: shield + stamina + parry.
    - **Mina**: almost no traditional block; relies on **spacing, knockback, burrow i-frames**.
- **Health management**
    - **ALttP/LA/Zelda NES**: hearts from drops + potions + maybe a single “auto-revive” (Secret Medicine/Fairy).
    - **Tunic**: Estus-style potion + hearts from drops.
    - **Prodigal**: heavy stacking of food buffs, revives, and Boon/Lovely Meal.
    - **Mina**: **Bloodborne-style regain** (hit back to reclaim recent damage) + vials.

**Takeaway:**  
Classic Zeldas have **simple swing/block systems**; newer games build complex combat identities with **stamina, i-frames, parries, regain, and build-defining passives.**

## 19. Exploration, Puzzles & Meta Systems

**Shared patterns:**

- **Environmental secrets**:
    - **Bomb walls** (Zelda NES, ALttP, LA), **burn bushes** (Zelda NES), **push blocks/statues** (Zelda NES, LA), **cut grass/bushes** (LA), etc.
- **Phone hints / NPC hints / manual pages**:
    - **LA - Phone booths**, **Prodigal/Mina - NPCs & Underlab**, **Tunic - Manual pages** as meta puzzle layer.
- **Mini-games & side activities**:
    - **LA - fishing, Trendy Game, raft races, digging**.
    - **Prodigal - fishing, mining, Crocasino, arenas, romances**.
    - **IoR - bug catching, fishing** (final release).

**Singularly unique:**

- **ALttP - Light World ↔ Dark World**: entire overworld duplicated in two states, with **mirror-based routing puzzles**.
- **IoR - Fairy Copy/Spawn System**: copy objects/tiles/torches/mushrooms and paste them, cross-room copy, tile-bridging; this is a **fully unique world-editing mechanic**.
- **Mina - Hollowing/burrow + checkpoint digging**: terrain layer + secret routing + checkpoint reveals.
- **Tunic - Manual as actual game mechanic**: discovery of rules, controls, and secrets via collection of **manual pages**.
