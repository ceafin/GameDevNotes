## E. A Specifically Gaelic Meta-Mechanic: Geasa as System

This isn’t a direct archetype from the file lists, but it _emerges_ logically from:
- Passive trinkets & rings,
- Temporary buffs / taboos,
- Story macguffins & quest conditions.

In Irish and Scottish myth, **geis / geasa** are personal taboos/obligations that bring power when obeyed and ruin when broken.
### How to turn _geasa_ into a mechanic archetype for Nasc

**Purpose / Traits**
- Long-term **build modifiers** and **story contracts** rather than simple buffs.
- They are **opt-in**: you choose them via shrines, NPC bargains, or equipping certain relics.
- They carry **mechanical power** _and_ **roleplay constraints** (e.g. can’t kill certain beings, must help travelers, must answer any riddle asked).

**For Nasc**
- Treat **Geasa** as a **MUST-HAVE IDENTITY SYSTEM** for Finsceal Bhríde – this is what makes Nasc feel _Gaelic_ rather than just "Zelda in Irish clothes"
- Implementation sketch:
    - Equip up to 2–3 **Geas slots**
    - Each geis is tied to a relic or blessing (Brigid’s forge, a Sídhe contract, a Manx water-spirit, etc.)
    - UI shows the **benefit** and the **taboo condition**
    - Violating it in a dungeon triggers a **curse meter** (more dangerous enemies, Sluagh spawning winds, XP loss)

**Gaelic-specific examples**

1. **Geis of Hospitality**
    - _Buff_: Bonus healing when near hearths; merchants discount prices.
    - _Taboo_: You must offer help to any peaceful NPC you meet (if you skip a quest hook or refuse, the geis cracks).
2. **Geis of the Hound** (echoing Cú Chulainn’s dog-geis conflict)   
    - _Buff_: Increased damage vs monsters, bonus roll i-frames.
    - _Taboo_: Cannot willingly harm **dogs / hounds / wolf-dogs**; doing so gives a stacking curse.
3. **Geis of the Tide-Horse** (Manx cabbyl-ushtey / water-horse)   
    - _Buff_: Swim speed up; no knockback in water; water-foes slower.
    - _Taboo_: Never turn your back to deep water in combat (if you roll away from an enemy _toward_ land, you’re fine; roll toward the shore with back to water – violation → ghostly tide surges).
4. **Geis of Bríd’s Fire**
    - _Buff_: Fire spells and bomb damage increased; torch radius larger.
    - _Taboo_: You must **never let your lantern go out inside a dungeon** (if it does, Bríd’s fire “abandons” you until you atone at a shrine).   

You can layer geasa onto **trinkets, quests, and story beats** so that _mechanics, narrative, and Gaelic flavor all point in the same direction._

## TL;DR – Core Must-Have Set for _Finsceal Bhríde_

If you want a Zelda-classic feel with strong Gaelic identity, I’d treat these as **non-negotiable pillars**:

1. **Primary melee line** rooted in Brigidine forging (with at least one “light/beam” tier).
2. **Returning stun tool** (Ogham stone or word-bound weapon).
3. **Bombs from Bríd’s eternal fire** + simple ranged precision tool (rod/bow).
4. **Shield + light defensive system** flavored as Bríd’s cloak/cross.
5. **Movement triad**: boots, at least one jump/air assist, water traversal (with water-horse folklore driving the threat).
6. **Strength / lifting gauntlets** as Fían-style trials.
7. **Shrine-based stat growth and small, meaningful passive trinkets**.
8. **Keys/maps/macguffins** themed as Brigidine relics and verses.
9. **Warp network via wells + a panic recall charm.**
10. **Geasa as a global meta-system**, binding Nasc to taboos that are both buffs and narrative hooks.

Everything else (burrow/Sídhe layer, IoR-style copying, elaborate food buffs, deep buildcraft) is **optional riff space** – places to scale scope up or down depending on what you actually have time and energy to build.
