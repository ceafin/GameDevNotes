### Early ranged pest – **Octorok / Zora-lite**  
  
Cheap, readable, early-game threats, often tied to waterlines.  
  
- **Merrow (Muir-Ógh)**  
    - **Role:** Stationary or side-stepping shoreline shooter.  
    - **Behavior:** Pops up from shallow water / rock outcrops, throws short arcing javelins or spits water bolts, ducks back down.  
    - **Use:** Your “Octorok on coasts / lake edges.”  
- **Dobhar-chú**  
    - **Role:** More aggressive Octorok variant.  
    - **Behavior:** Surfaces, spits a fast projectile, then rushes in to bite before diving again.  
    - **Use:** Later in the same progression where the player already understands basic “water shooter.”  
- **Fuath**  
    - **Role:** Bridge / river ambush.  
    - **Behavior:** Lurks under bridges; surfaces to lob a slime/curse blob then retreats.  
    - **Use:** “Octorok but in tight overworld chokepoints.”  
  
---  
  
### Basic melee grunt – **Moblin / Bokoblin**  
  
Low–mid-tier humanoid enemies, often in groups.  
  
- **Fir Bolg Marauder**  
    - **Role:** Mainline grunt enemy with spear, axe, or club.  
    - **Behavior:** Patrols in pairs, simple chase + swing, occasionally throws a spear.  
    - **Use:** Your primary “Moblin” faction, readable as human-ish raiders.  
- **Fomoire Spawn**  
    - **Role:** Mutated, armored variant for dungeons.  
    - **Behavior:** Slightly slower, tougher, with occasional shield blocks or heavy swing windups.  
    - **Use:** Dungeon “Bokoblin+” in Fomorian-flavored areas.  
- **Aos Sí (Sidhe host – footsoldier)**  
    - **Role:** Fairy equivalent of Moblin spearmen / bowmen.  
    - **Behavior:** More evasive, uses short-range blink and ranged shots or spear thrusts.  
    - **Use:** “Trickier Moblins” in fairy mounds and groves.  
  
---  
  
### Armored elite – **Darknut / Iron Knuckle**  
  
Slow-ish, high-damage, pattern-based elites.  
  
- **Fomorian Foot-Soldier (elite)**  
    - **Role:** Your straight Darknut analog.  
    - **Behavior:** Heavy shield, telegraphed swings, directional guarding, punishable back / side.  
    - **Use:** Late-dungeon room anchors, key-guarders, and “oh no” overworld elites near strongholds.  
- **Manx Barrow Wight**  
    - **Role:** Skeletal Darknut / Stalfos Knight hybrid.  
    - **Behavior:** Shield + sword, can feign death and rise again if not burned / blessed.  
    - **Use:** As a recurring elite and also upgradeable to a small-dungeon boss.  
- **Holed-Stone Warden**  
    - **Role:** Statuesque, sliding Darknut/Armos hybrid.  
    - **Behavior:** Slides along grid lines, charges beams through its eye-hole, high defense frontally.  
    - **Use:** Especially good in puzzle-heavy, stone-circle dungeons.  
  
---  
  
### Annoying late-game casters – **Wizzrobe**  
  
Teleporting magic users that punish inattentive play.  
  
- **Aos Sí Caster**  
    - **Role:** Baseline Wizzrobe.  
    - **Behavior:** Short-range blink, fires elemental bolts (wind / light), vanishes.  
    - **Use:** Standard magical pest once player has decent mobility tools.  
- **Leanan Sídhe**  
    - **Role:** Advanced Wizzrobe that drains magic/HP.  
    - **Behavior:** Appears near “creative” NPCs or relics, fires draining rays, can tether to the player to sap MP and hearts.  
    - **Use:** Mini-arc enemies in artist shrines, bard halls, etc.  
- **Gancanagh**  
    - **Role:** Control-focused caster.  
    - **Behavior:** Charms NPCs or summons enthralled mobs, pulses confusion / reverse-controls spells.  
    - **Use:** Late-dungeon Wizzrobe that screws with controls rather than just damage.  
- **Baobhan sìth**  
    - **Role:** High-threat Wizzrobe-plus miniboss.  
    - **Behavior:** Teleports in as beautiful woman, drags you into “dance” radius that drains life; summons adds.  
    - **Use:** Key miniboss for fairy courts / haunted glens.  
  
---  
  
### Overworld superboss – **Lynel**  
  
Fast, lethal, multi-phase overworld fights.  
  
- **Sídhe Boar or Stag**  
    - **Role:** Your basic Lynel slot.  
    - **Behavior:** Charges, uses short-range magical shockwave on stomp, fires antler or tusk projectiles.  
    - **Use:** Roaming “do not engage early” elites in forests / moors.  
- **Torc Triath (Otherworld boar)**  
    - **Role:** Named Lynel-tier.  
    - **Behavior:** Same as above but with multi-phase fight, enraged second phase, maybe summons ghost-hounds.  
    - **Use:** Story-linchpin boss of a seasonal hunt arc.  
- **Heather-Field Stag Spirit**  
    - **Role:** Optional “glass cannon” Lynel-type.  
    - **Behavior:** Teleporting, phasing stag that goredashes and then fades out, leaving smaller spirit deer.  
    - **Use:** Sidequest superboss in heather moors.  
  
---  
  
### Big brute miniboss – **Hinox**  
  
One-on-one punchy fights.  
  
- **Grogoch / Fenodyree**  
    - **Role:** First “huge brute” many players meet.  
    - **Behavior:** Slow but hits like a truck, rocks or tree trunks as projectiles, short enrages.  
    - **Use:** Farmstead / barn ruins miniboss.  
- **Buggane**  
    - **Role:** Fortified Hinox variant.  
    - **Behavior:** Smashes walls/pillars, creates shockwaves, maybe headless variant later.  
    - **Use:** Bridge/church ruin miniboss or early boss.  
- **Fachan**  
    - **Role:** Hyper-aggressive one-eyed beast.  
    - **Behavior:** High movement speed, big leaping slam, goes berserk when low HP.  
    - **Use:** Mountain or cliffside miniboss that punishes greedy players.  
  
---  
  
### Standard undead melee – **Stalfos / Stalfos Knight**  
  
Reliable skeleton/undead sword fighters.  
  
- **Corpse-Host / Marbh Beo**  
    - **Role:** Stalfos fodder.  
    - **Behavior:** Slow or shuffling basic melee with predictable swings.  
    - **Use:** Crypts, plague-villages, cursed battlefields.  
- **Marbhán Bog-Dead**  
    - **Role:** Environmental Stalfos.  
    - **Behavior:** Half-buried; rise if stepped near, then fight like sluggish, mud-weighted Stalfos.  
    - **Use:** Swamp/bog areas and dungeons.  
- **Manx Barrow Wight**  
    - **Role:** Knight-tier Stalfos.  
    - **Behavior:** Shield and advanced moveset; second-phase resurrection.  
  
---  
  
### Horror/paralysis undead – **ReDead / Gibdo**  
  
Lockdown horror enemies.  
  
- **Abhartach**  
    - **Role:** “Named ReDead” miniboss or boss.  
    - **Behavior:** Paralysis scream or gaze, repeated resurrection; drains life on grab.  
- **Abhartach-Style Blood Shade**  
    - **Role:** Moving curse hazard.  
    - **Behavior:** Creeps along walls/floor, paralyzes on contact, sets up Abhartach’s fights.  
- **Dearg-Due**  
    - **Role:** Mobile ReDead.  
    - **Behavior:** Fast grab attempts, lifedrain, spawns cursed hearts that hurt when picked up.  
- **Luideag**  
    - **Role:** Corridor ReDead.  
    - **Behavior:** Very slow but phases through doors and walls, drains stamina when nearby.  
  
---  
  
### Grab traps – **Wallmaster / Floormaster**  
  
The “don’t stand still here” teachers.  
  
- **Lámh Dhearg (“red hand” ghost)**  
    - **Role:** Wall / ceiling grabber.  
    - **Behavior:** Drops or slides in from screen edges, drags you back or to some cursed cell.  
- **Lámh Ghortaigh (“afflicting hand”)**  
    - **Role:** Floor snare.  
    - **Behavior:** Grabs ankles, slows, applies debuff (reduced defense / poison / famine).  
  
---  
  
### Animated statues – **Armos**  
  
Obstacle → enemy reveals.  
  
- **Holed-Stone Warden (small forms)**  
    - **Role:** Armos troops.  
    - **Behavior:** Look like inert standing stones; wake up when interacted with, charge and body-check.  
- **Crom Cruach small serpent-idols**  
    - **Role:** Armos variants.  
    - **Behavior:** Hop or slither when activated, spit short poison jets.  
  
---  
  
### Gear-eaters / thieves – **Like Like**  
  
Things that steal your stuff.  
  
- **Púca**  
    - **Role:** Like-Like with guile.  
    - **Behavior:** Appears as harmless animal (goat, horse, dog); if you interact carelessly it “swallows” equipment (temporarily removing shield / weapon until beaten).  
- **Leprechaun**  
    - **Role:** Rupee thief.  
    - **Behavior:** Appears, taunts, then dashes in to steal rupees or consumables, vanishing into fairy rings.  
  
---  
  
### Flying pests – **Keese / Peahat / Tektite**  
  
Light fliers or jumpers for vertical pressure.  
  
- **Badb (battle crows)** and **Storm-Cailleach’s Crows**  
    - **Role:** Keese analogs.  
    - **Behavior:** Flock behavior, swoop and retreat, can strip armor or peck magic.  
- **Corpse-Lights (Soilse Marbha)**  
    - **Role:** Peahat-lite.  
    - **Behavior:** Drift, then suddenly dash; immune when bright, vulnerable when dim.  
- **Sídhe Whirlwind**  
    - **Role:** Peahat/mini-tornado hybrid.  
    - **Behavior:** Drifts across terrain, knocking you back; sometimes carries smaller enemies.

## 2. Starter roster for the first region  
  
Assume:  
  
- **World layout:** One big contiguous region made of  
    - **Island A** – mild, green, late-spring / early-summer climate.  
    - **Island B** – more rugged and permanently late-autumn in certain groves and hills.  
    - **Scattered islets** – mostly coastal caves, mini-dungeons.  
- No global time system; instead, **pockets of season**:  
    - A “Samhain grove” that’s _always_ autumn night.  
    - A central bog that feels perma-late autumn.  
    - Coastal areas that are always stormy or always calm.  
  
### 2.1 Overworld enemies (8)  
  
For the first region, keep it readable and not too cruel. These are what the player sees roaming the overworld.  
  
1. **Sídhe Hare** – _fields & meadow edges (spring/summer pocket)_  
    - **Slot:** Light Peahat/Tektite nuisance.  
    - **Behavior:** Teleports short distances when hit or targeted; darts in to nibble then blinks away.  
    - **Use:** Teaches the player to respect unpredictable movement and aim.  
2. **Geilt** – _deeper woods & rocky slopes on Island A/B_  
    - **Slot:** Unarmored Bokoblin-lite.  
    - **Behavior:** Skittish feral human; will throw stones or charge with a stick, then flee if badly hurt.  
    - **Use:** Adds “humanoid but tragic” layer to early combat, not just beasts.  
3. **Leprechaun** – _hedgerows, crossroads, ruined walls_  
    - **Slot:** Rupee thief (Like-Like variant).  
    - **Behavior:** Taunts from a distance, then dash-grabs rupees/items and hides in a fairy ring; can be chased down.  
    - **Use:** Teaches paying attention to economy and not mindlessly chasing every shiny.  
4. **Corpse-Lights (Soilse Marbha)** – _central bog & low misty fields_  
    - **Slot:** Floating hazard / light Peahat.  
    - **Behavior:** Drift in patterns over safe ground, then lure you toward deep bog tiles or ambush spots.  
    - **Use:** Teaches reading terrain and not following lights blindly.  
5. **Cú Glas / Moddey Dhoo** – _roads, old forts, bridges at night_  
    - **Slot:** Slightly tougher overworld melee.  
    - **Behavior:** Patrols, occasionally phases briefly to dodge attacks; gains damage bonus at night.  
    - **Use:** First “night is worse” signal, but localized to certain routes.  
6. **Dobhar-chú** – _river mouths, lakeshores, sea inlets_  
    - **Slot:** Octorok-adjacent waterline enemy.  
    - **Behavior:** Surfaces to spit a fast shot, then dives or lunges to bite at close range.  
    - **Use:** Teaches ranged attacks from water and shoreline positioning.  
7. **Púca** – _country lanes and farm edges_  
    - **Slot:** Like-Like-style trickster.  
    - **Behavior:** Appears as a friendly animal; if mounted or over-interacted with, steals an equipped item (shield or secondary) and runs off.  
    - **Use:** Reinforces mistrust of “too friendly” entities in wilds; low damage, high annoyance.  
8. **Oíche Shamhna Spirits** – _a fixed “Samhain grove” on Island B (perma-autumn dusk)_  
    - **Slot:** Keese/Bubbles-style area-specific ghosts.  
    - **Behavior:** Drift in from the background, pass through terrain, inflict curse / minor HP drain.  
    - **Use:** Signals the seasonal theme: this patch of forest is _always_ Samhain-night.  
  
---  
  
### 2.2 Dungeon enemies (8)  
  
Assume two early dungeons in the region:  
  
- **Dungeon 1:** A **barrow / crypt** on Island A.  
- **Dungeon 2:** A **flooded cave / lake-side ruin** between A and B (entry to Muirdris).  
  
1. **Corpse-Host / Marbh Beo** – _barrow_  
    - **Slot:** Stalfos fodder.  
    - **Behavior:** Basic undead with slow swings; some rise from piles of bones.  
    - **Use:** Core melee enemy of Dungeon 1.  
2. **Marbhán Bog-Dead** – _lower levels of barrow & swampy tunnels_  
    - **Slot:** Terrain-tied Stalfos.  
    - **Behavior:** Half-buried; only upper torso emerges to claw. Dies back into mud, can respawn if room is reset.  
3. **Fuath** – _flooded corridors, wells, bridge-rooms_  
    - **Slot:** Wet Octorok with ambush angle.  
    - **Behavior:** Only visible eyes in the water; pops up to spit or grab ankles, then submerges.  
4. **Rowan-Root Creeper** – _entrance to a sacred grove chamber & tree-lined passages_  
    - **Slot:** Environmental trap enemy.  
    - **Behavior:** Roots animate when you linger; pull you toward spike pits / hazards.  
5. **Lámh Dhearg** – _ceilings in key rooms and chokepoints_  
    - **Slot:** Wallmaster equivalent.  
    - **Behavior:** Shadow forms on ground first; hand drops to grab and drag you back to entrance or side-cell.  
6. **Luideag (“ragged one”)** – _tight corridors and dead ends_  
    - **Slot:** ReDead-lite horror.  
    - **Behavior:** Slow, persistent, phases through low obstacles, drains stamina and reduces movement speed if close.  
7. **Aos Sí footsoldier (spear/bow)** – _central mound-like chamber inside the barrow_  
    - **Slot:** Smarter Moblin.  
    - **Behavior:** Uses short-range blink, can reposition behind you, uses spears or small bows.  
8. **Merrow** – _underwater or semi-flooded chambers in Dungeon 2_  
    - **Slot:** Stronger waterline Octorok / Zora analog.  
    - **Behavior:** Throws tridents / bolts from rock perches, can dive and reappear at different perches in the same room.  
  
---  
  
### 2.3 Minibosses (3)  
  
These should feel memorable but not total brick walls.  
  
1. **Grogoch / Fenodyree** – _abandoned farmstead or ruined hill-barn on Island A_  
    - **Slot:** First Hinox-style fight.  
    - **Behavior:** Slow but wide club swings, rock throws, enrages at low HP (faster charge).  
    - **Narrative:** Might be a once-helpful spirit turned sour; beating him could cleanse the farm.  
2. **Cú Sídhe (fairy hound)** – _deep grove on Island A or B, guarding a fairy well_  
    - **Slot:** Mobile elite guard (Darknut-lite Lynel).  
    - **Behavior:** Fast dash, shockwave bark, short teleport lunges.  
    - **Narrative:** Defeat or appease to gain access to a fairy well / major item.  
3. **Heather-Field Stag Spirit** – _open moor on the higher side of Island B_  
    - **Slot:** Optional Lynel-style overworld miniboss.  
    - **Behavior:** Phases out after charges, can only be hit during certain tells; drops rare antler/essence.  
    - **Narrative:** Early hint of “Otherworld hunt” themes and of much nastier future beasts.  
  
---  
  
### 2.4 Bosses (2)  
  
Two bosses that are strong but still “first region” tier.  
  
1. **Manx Barrow Wight** – _Boss of Dungeon 1 (barrow / crypt)_  
    - **Slots filled:** Darknut + Stalfos Knight + “first serious boss.”  
    - **Phase ideas:**  
        - **Phase 1:** Standard armored undead knight with shield; teaches reading telegraphs and attacking from flanks.  
        - **Phase 2:** Armor shatters; Wight becomes faster, uses short-range curses and teleport dashes between sarcophagi.  
    - **Narrative hook:** Defender of an old line of kings or chieftains; defeating him either calms the line or breaks a taboo, which can echo into later regions.  
2. **Muirdris** – _Boss of Dungeon 2 (flooded cave / lakeside ruin between the islands)_  
    - **Slots filled:** Aquatic beast boss, proto-“lake dragon.”  
    - **Phase ideas:**  
        - **Phase 1:** Only the head/neck surface from circular lake arena; spits projectiles, tail sweeps underwater.  
        - **Phase 2:** Clambers partially onto platforms, face-twisting roar that temporarily distorts UI / controls.  
    - **Narrative hook:** Its gaze is rumored to warp faces; villagers are terrified of the lake. Killing or sparing Muirdris could affect how people see the player (literally or metaphorically).  
  
---  
  
### 2.5 Seasonal theming in the first region  
  
Without a global calendar, you can still give strong seasonal flavor:  
  
- **Island A – “Greenstrand” (perma late spring / early summer)**  
    - Overworld: Sídhe Hare, Geilt, Leprechaun, Púca, occasional Cú Glas.  
    - Dungeon 1 (barrow): Corpse-Host, Marbhán Bog-Dead, Luideag, Lámh Dhearg, Aos Sí footsoldiers.  
- **Island B – “Autumnfell” (perma late autumn in interior)**  
    - Outer coasts: Dobhar-chú, some Geilt and Cú Glas at night.  
    - Interior Samhain grove: Oíche Shamhna Spirits, Corpse-Lights, maybe a Luideag or two in overworld ruins.  
    - Higher moor: Heather-Field Stag Spirit as optional miniboss.  
- **Scattered islets & lake ruin**  
    - Dungeon 2 (Muirdris’ flooded cave): Fuath, Merrow, Rowan-Root Creeper near entrances.  
    - Small islets: little Púca tricks, Dobhar-chú packs, maybe a single Grogoch/Fenodyree on a “training” island.
